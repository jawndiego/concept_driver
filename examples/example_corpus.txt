January is often cold, while July is warm. February can be snowy, and August is peak summer. March begins the transition into spring, while September starts the transition into autumn. April brings rain, and October brings falling leaves. November feels late autumn, and December closes the year with winter holidays. June and July are usually the hottest months, and January and February are often the coldest.

Monday starts the work week, Tuesday follows, and Wednesday sits in the middle. Thursday comes before Friday, and Saturday and Sunday make up the weekend. People often look forward to Friday, recover on Saturday, and rest on Sunday before Monday returns.

Spring brings blossoms, summer brings heat, autumn brings harvest, and winter brings snow. Red and orange often suggest warmth, yellow suggests sunlight, green suggests plants, blue suggests sky or sea, and purple suggests dusk.
